Social Media Best Practices for College Students
The "DOs" (Positive Actions)
Audit Your Privacy: Regularly check your security settings to ensure only intended audiences can see your personal posts.

Build a Professional Brand: Use platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your projects, certifications, and academic milestones.

Think Before You Post: Ask yourself if you’d be comfortable with a future hiring manager or a grandmother seeing the content.

Network Purposefully: Follow industry leaders and engage with content related to your major to stay informed.

Verify Your Sources: Check the credibility of a story or "news" post before sharing it to avoid spreading misinformation.

The "DON'Ts" (Risky Behaviors)
Don't Post Illegal Content: Avoid sharing photos or videos involving underage drinking, drug use, or reckless behavior.

Don't Badmouth Institutions: Never vent about your university, professors, or current/past employers on public forums.

Don't Share Sensitive Data: Keep your student ID number, home address, and daily schedule off your public feed.

Don't Engage with Trolls: Avoid getting baited into heated online arguments or using offensive language, as these can be screenshotted and saved.

Don't Forget the "Delete" Rule: Remember that "deleted" doesn't mean "gone." Digital footprints are often permanent via archives or screenshots.
